A Rant About Audio Formats

C/U/ID: 2023-04-12/2023-04-19/P001

Note: I no longer consider this post accurate to my opinions.

Earlier today I spent around two hours looking for a website to download higher quality music from. I first tried out yt-dlp, but I got tired of fuddling with that after using the remux option refused to work. I then found a site that allowed you to download FLAC files. However, upon throwing these FLAC files into Foobar, I found that I honestly could not tell the difference between the newly downloaded FLACs and the MP3s I already had in my library.

Now, I don't know if it's just my headphones, or me not being an 'audiophile', but I think too many people fuss about audio formats. For the vast majority of people, streaming quality is going to be perfectly fine. Personally, I think songs on YouTube are of perfectly listenable quality; I haven't used Spotify, but I'm sure it's the same story. Most people don't have super fancy headphones and most people listen to music casually. The only instance I can see where one would need high quality FLACs is if you have time that you dedicate solely to listening to music.

Next time someone shames you for listening to lossy formats or streaming, just remember that they are probably way more into music than you. Your collection of MP3s or favorited Spotify tracks is probably good enough for you, and you aren't really missing out. On top of that, it's really hard to find music downloads that aren't streaming rips or cost money, anyway.
