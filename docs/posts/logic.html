There's a Certain Logic to It

C/U/ID: 2023-06-02/2023-06-04/P002

Preface

This is a little post about people not thinking logically when they should.

Did you just say...?

Chances are, if it was so unreasonable that you had to ask, then no, I probably didn't say that. I think in a lot of instances, this line of questioning comes from an attempt at humor, but I've never been in a situation where it didn't fall flat. At school, I am a very reserved person who doesn't say a lot of vulgar things, so it baffles me when people mishear me and go "did you just say [thing you would never say at school]?" What these people don't understand is that people, like many things, have a certain logic to them.

Not to get all Vulcan on you, but people generally follow some sort of logical system, even though it obviously varies from person to person. People have a range of personally acceptable behaviors, and generally don't engage in behaviors outside of that system besides the occassional exceptional circumstance. For example, anyone that knows me a bit knows I don't like sex jokes. I've never found them funny and they often just make me uncomfortable. So when people mishear me and think I've said something sexual, they're thinking outside of my system. Again, this obviously varies from person to person, and the boundaries are very fuzzy and only gradually come into focus as you get to know someone better.

The point is, if you've misunderstood someone, assume they're operating within their system as you ask them to clarify. Instead of asking "did you just say [unreasonable thing]?", simply ask them to repeat themselves. Then you can accurately determine if what they said was truly unreasonable.

Damn computer!

Computers, even more so than people, follow logical systems. Excusing the occassional true bug, most software works as intended if operated properly. My issue is when people blame the computer or the software for something that's clearly user error to someone more experienced. To be fair, it's expected. When one misses a strike with the hammer, is one more likely to blame oneself for aiming the strike wrong or blame the weight of the hammer being off? However, it doesn't make it any less frustrating as an observer, especially when people resort to hitting the computer, which can only make things work. Combined with most people's general stubbornness, it's very hard to help people realize what they're doing wrong, although not impossible.

Part of the issue, I believe, is a general lack in understanding of how computers work. Many people blame "lag" for their computer issues. But is that network slowness? Display delay? Hardware hinderence? (It's usually the network, but that doesn't stop one of my classmates from blaming the "HDTV lag", which I can't even begin to properly dissect.) Often one does not bother to check, because the situation resolves itself in mere seconds.

For non-"lag" related issues, the user often gives up without asking for help. Many a classmate of mine has complained about Word not doing something they want, only for me to find out they have it formatted completely wrong. These people were required to take a class on Microsoft Office and basic IT knowledge, by the way. But of course, they blame the software.

Conclusion

I think the world would be just a little better if people took a second to think about things a little more logically. Obviously, that's not always possible, and we're not trying to recreate Vulcan here, but a lot of confusion and misinformation could be negated with a tad more logic.
