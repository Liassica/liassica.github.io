Nomoxapine® vs Conversion Therapy

Important Safety Information

Do not take Nomoxapine® if you are allergic to it or any of its ingredients.

What is Nomoxapine®?

Nomoxapine® (ventatracin) is a once-daily medicine used:

  • along with social support and therapy to reduce feelings of homosexual thoughts in adolescents and adults
  • to reduce the need for frequent conversion therapy sessions

The effectiveness of Nomoxapine® in children under 12 has not been tested.

What are the possible side effects of Nomoxapine®?

  • Common side effects of Nomoxapine® include: nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, fatigue, headaches, and rash.
  • In rare cases, Nomoxapine® may cause depression or suicidal thoughts.

Discuss with your healthcare provider if you encounter any side effects.

You are encouraged to report negative side effects of prescription drugs to the FDA. Visit www.fda.gov/medwatch, or call 1-800-FDA-1088.

Before starting Nomoxapine, tell your healthcare provider:

  • any medications you are on, including vitamins, supplements, over-the-counter medicines, and other prescriptions
  • any existing medical conditions
  • if you or your family has history of bowel conditions such as ulcerative colitis or Crohn's disease
  • if you have or suspect you have tuberculosis (TB) or recently come into contact with someone with TB
